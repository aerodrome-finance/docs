# Tokenomics

Aerodrome operates as a zero-leak economy where 100% of fees are distributed to users of the system: the liquidity providers who deposit assets that make low-slippage trades possible, and the token operators who coordinate and optimize the flow of liquidity.

Liquidity providers (LPs) on Aerodrome can choose to stake their deposit in emissions-eligible pools to earn AERO (Aerodrome's native token), or leave their deposit unstaked to earn a proportional share of trading fees. Historically, the vast majority of LPs have chosen to stake liquidity to earn maximum rewards.

The fees generated by unstaked liquidity do not accrue to the protocol as revenue, but are passed through to LPs directly. The only exception is that 10% of fees earned by unstaked liquidity in emissions-eligible Slipstream pools is captured as a platform fee and routed to veAERO as revenue.

In addition to the protocol share of unstaked trading fees, Aerodrome accrues 100% of fees from staked liquidity + all voting incentives as revenue. This protocol revenue is internalized and distributed to token operators, a decentralized network of participants who have locked AERO to participate in governance. 

This is a diverse stakeholder group comprised of institutional investors, protocols, retail traders, and more—any and all tokenholders on equal footing, earning according to their participation without privilege or preference. 

The Aerodrome Foundation, the protocol’s neutral steward, and Dromos Labs, the core contributing development company, both earn revenue exclusively through participation in this system. These entities were granted max-locked voting positions at launch, locks which are used to vote each week to earn revenue. 

The Aerodrome Foundation uses its share of revenue to strengthen the ecosystem with efforts like token buybacks and incentive deposits. Dromos Labs uses its share to compensate contributors and fund ongoing development. To ensure long-term competitiveness, Dromos Labs is also allotted a small percentage of weekly AERO emissions (100% of which is compounded into the max-locked position).

This token-centric economic system aligns the interests of all stakeholders—LPs, traders, and tokenholders—to help Aerodrome maintain maximum efficiency and sustainability for the long term.

---

Aerodrome Finance uses two tokens to manage its utility and governance:

- `$AERO` &mdash; ERC-20 utility token of the protocol
- `$veAERO` &mdash; ERC-721 governance token in the form of an NFT
  (non-fungible token)

`$AERO` is distributed to liquidity providers through emissions.

`$veAERO` is used for governance. Any `$AERO` holder can vote-escrow their
tokens and receive a `$veAERO` (also known as Lock or veNFT) in exchange.
Additional tokens can be added to the `$veAERO` NFT at any time.

The lock period (also known as vote-escrowed period, hence the _ve_ prefix) can
be up to 4 years, following the linear relationship shown below:

- 100 `$AERO` locked for 4 years will become 100 `$veAERO`
- 100 `$AERO` locked for 1 year will become 25 `$veAERO`

The longer the vesting time, the higher the voting power (voting weight) of the
underlying locked balance.

Additionally, Aerodrome Locks (veNFTs) can be set into Auto-Max Lock, which are
treated by the protocol as being locked for the maximum duration of 4 years,
and their voting power does not decay. The Auto-Max Lock feature can be turned
on and off for each Lock (veNFT).
